Our Story

Born from the Field,
Built for Impact

AbXInsight wasn't created in a boardroom. It was built in the hospitals and ministries of the countries that need it most.

2018 - The Beginning

A Gap in the System

Working with WHO and the Fleming Fund across Southeast Asia, we saw the same problem everywhere: countries wanted to monitor antimicrobial consumption and had a strong desire to report to WHO or WOAH, but there were no easy, user-friendly tools available. Existing solutions were too complex, with long delays between dispensing and reporting. Excel spreadsheets were the norm.

1

"We had the methodology, we had the WHO guidelines, but no accessible tool to implement them at scale."

"Hospitals want to contribute to national surveillance, but there's a disconnect between private and public sectors."

2
2024 - A New Realisation

Bridging the Gap

We discovered that hospitals ‚Äî both public and private ‚Äî genuinely want to contribute to national surveillance efforts. But there was no easy way to connect them. We set out to bridge that gap with seamless hospital-to-national data integration.

2025 - Rising to the Challenge

When the World Needs Us Most

WHO is having to do more with less. The Fleming Fund is ending. But the needs are greater than ever. That's when we decided: let's step up and help countries continue their surveillance efforts. With AI now omnipresent, we can analyse data faster and bring clinical guidelines to life ‚Äî making stewardship actionable, not just theoretical.

2025 - The Reality

A Global Gap

According to TRACSS (Global Monitoring), nearly 20% of countries worldwide have no national antibiotic use tracking system, and another 28% have very limited capacity. That's almost half the world flying blind on antibiotic use.
