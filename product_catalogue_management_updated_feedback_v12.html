AbXInsight Product Catalogue Management

This mockup includes Screens 6 to 8 and focuses on building and maintaining the tenant product catalogue, including Excel upload seeding. The catalogue is tenant-owned. In Pro, hospitals in the same account share one catalogue. Pro does not include national catalogue search or adoption. In Ultra, hospitals can choose to adopt the National List or maintain their own list.

Quickfox note: Hospital and national codes are user-managed and never auto-generated. In Pro, hospitals share a single tenant catalogue, but there is no national catalogue search or adoption. Show hospital local product codes (for example SKU-001) and national codes (where available) alongside the AbX canonical Product ID. Reuse of the same local product code indicates additional consumption and must be handled in consumption entry, not by duplicating catalogue items.

How the product catalogue supports data entry

AbXInsight uses a stable internal key (product_uid) and a human-readable canonical ID (abx_product_id). Hospitals and countries keep their own codes as scoped external IDs. These codes are never auto-generated by the system.

Basic
Hospitals search and enter data using their own codes and product names.
Pro
Optional: Pro includes a single shared tenant catalogue across hospitals in the same account. Pro tenants build up the catalogue themselves, but it is shared by all users on the same account.
Ultra
Optional: ability to enforce or strongly default national codes for selected workflows.

Rule: if the same hospital code is used again, it means additional consumption of the same product. Adjust quantities in consumption entry, not the catalogue.

Catalogue sharing rule

Product catalogues are not shared between accounts. Within a single account, sharing depends on tier.

  • Basic: hospital builds and uses its own catalogue
  • Pro: all hospitals in the same Pro account share one catalogue
  • Ultra: hospital can choose to adopt the National List or maintain its own list

What users enter in data entry

Users select a product from the catalogue, then enter quantities. Route, strength, pack size, and DDD are pulled from the catalogue automatically.
